About Us

Our Mission
Our mission at Timeless Massage is to inspire and delight our guests through the healing power of massage. We are committed to making a difference and improving every day, ensuring that we consistently offer clients our very best.
The Founder
The owner and founder of Timeless Massage has studied massage since her adolescence when she became intrigued by the healing powers of bodywork and acupuncture. Since then, she has sought out extensive training in China, Thailand, Canada, and the United States, with emphasis on lymphatic drainage and sports medicine. She has traveled the world to service clients, employing a diverse range of skill sets to make sure each client is completely satisfied. She is dedicated to giving her team the proper training necessary for performing the specialty massages of Timeless Massage. Here in Las Vegas, she has enjoyed working for clients at the Aria, Bellagio, Caesars Palace, Cosmopolitan, MGM Grand, and The Mansion. Now she can bring her years of expertise and experiences from around the world to your door.
Our Team
The massage therapists of Timeless Massage are all professionally trained, certified, and licensed massage practitioners who have received the finest education offered in their field. Our superb bodywork staff is carefully screened using in-depth background checks, then selected for their technical skills and ability to offer only the highest level of healing to our clients.
Our Clients
A client of Timeless Massage will receive the utmost professional care and world class personal service. It’s why we are trusted with some of the highest profile and discerning clients in Las Vegas and beyond. We service clients at most Strip or Strip-adjacent properties, including but not limited to: Wynn/Encore, CityCenter, Caesars Palace, The Venetian/Palazzo, Planet Hollywood, and Cosmopolitan. Whether you are a professional athlete, CEO, or professional shopper with tired feet, our therapists will treat you with the same spirit of commitment and healing attention.
